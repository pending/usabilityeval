Aesthetics and Minimalist Design

Dialogues should not contain information which is irrelevant or rarely needed. Every extra unit of information in a dialogue competes with the relevant units of information and diminishes their relative visibility.

Some parts of the system mentioned above also provide operative complications to the overall aesthetic of the site. The duplication of features on a page increases the amount of visual stimulus users receive and consequently must process before accomplishing or completing their targetted task. The aesthetic choices made in allocating blue toned blurbs to link to additional alumni information and not to information regarding business enterprising opportunities adds both dimension and minimizes otherwise large bodies of related links. Moreover, the incorporation of a scrolling carousel provides users numerous shortcuts to different news columns at the expense of displaying unrelated information in clusters that could be optimized for increased memory (chunks of 5~7 mini blurbs).

Unfortunately, many of the subpages do not follow the design of the homepage. The subpages simply throw walls of links and text at the user, rather than organizing them in an aesthetically pleasing and managable manner.
